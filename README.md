# Life Expectancy and Economic Indicators Analysis

## Project Motivation

Life expectancy varies across countries due to many factors such as healthcare quality, economic development, and social conditions.  

The goal of this project is to explore whether economic indicators such as GDP per capita and healthcare expenditure are related to life expectancy and whether these variables can help predict life expectancy.

---

## Business Questions

1. What is the distribution of life expectancy?
2. Is GDP per capita related to life expectancy?
3. Does healthcare spending influence life expectancy?
4. Can economic indicators help predict life expectancy?

## Summary of Results

The exploratory data analysis suggests that GDP per capita and healthcare expenditure are positively related to life expectancy.

A linear regression model was built to predict life expectancy using economic indicators. The model achieved an R² score of approximately **0.31**, indicating that economic indicators explain part of the variation in life expectancy.
